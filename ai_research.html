Checkpoint 1

In this checkpoint we present our research questions about the results of the EIT/NMT.

  1. Is there a statistically significant difference between the average scores of men and women in basic subjects?
  2. Do students who took the test abroad have better results?
  3. Is there a correlation between results in different subjects?
  4. Which subjects are most often chosen as additional subjects (biology, chemistry, physics, etc.)?
  5. Is there statistical difference between results in different group ages(17/18 years)?
  6. What has a stronger impact on EIT/ NMT results: studying at a gymnasium/lyceum or the region of residence?
  7. Can we predict students' EIT/NMT scores based on school, region, settlement type, and demographic data?
  8. How has the war in Ukraine affected the educational outcomes of school graduates in different regions, including those affected by the war or temporarily occupied, compared to relatively safe regions?
  9. How has the demand for specific NMT/EІT subjects (such as foreign languages, physics, biology, geography, etc.) changed among participants in 2024 compared to previous years?
  10. Are there any noticeable trends in the increase or decrease in popularity of these subjects?
  11. Have student grades improved after the school changed from a gymnasium to a lyceum?
  12. How much better/worse did students do on the NMT than on the EIT?
  13. Do students who take fewer subjects have a higher average score?
